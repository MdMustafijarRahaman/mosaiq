Privacy Policy

Lumio: Block Puzzle

by Solvemash

Effective date: 24 June 2026  •  Last updated: 24 June 2026

In short. Lumio is a single‑player puzzle game with no account and no sign‑in. Your scores, settings and progress are stored only on your device and are never sent to us. The single third‑party service we use is Google AdMob, which shows ads to keep the game free and may collect device and advertising identifiers. We do not sell your data, and we serve non‑personalized ads by default unless you choose otherwise.

This Privacy Policy explains how the mobile application Lumio: Block Puzzle (the “App”, package com.solvemash.mosaiq), provided by Solvemash (“we”, “us”, or “our”), handles information when you use it. By installing or using the App, you agree to this Policy. If you do not agree, please do not use the App.

1. Who We Are

The App is developed and published by Solvemash, an independent developer. For any privacy question or request, contact us at solvemash@gmail.com. We are the data controller for the limited processing described in this Policy. Where ads are served, Google acts as an independent controller of the advertising data it collects (see Section 4).

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information you give us

None. The App has no registration, no login, no user profiles, and no in‑app messaging. We do not ask for your name, email, phone number, or any other contact or identity information to play.

2.2 Game data stored on your device

To let you play and pick up where you left off, the App saves the following locally on your device only (using on‑device storage). This information stays on your device, is not transmitted to our servers (we operate none), and is not accessible to us:

  • Best score, best streak, best combo, and other gameplay statistics;
  • Your current/last game state, so a closed game can resume;
  • Settings (sound, haptics, reduce‑motion, color‑blind option, selected theme, language);
  • Unlocked cosmetic themes, daily‑streak count, daily‑challenge results, and achievements;
  • Your advertising‑consent choice (so we can remember it).

Deleting the App removes this on‑device data. You can also clear it from within the App’s settings where available.

2.3 Information collected automatically for advertising

The App displays ads through Google AdMob. To serve, measure, and prevent fraud in advertising, Google and its ad partners may collect and process certain information through the App, including:

  • Device advertising identifier (e.g., Android Advertising ID) and other device identifiers;
  • Device and connection information (device model, operating‑system version, language, IP address, coarse/approximate location derived from IP, network information);
  • Ad‑interaction data (ads shown, taps, and similar diagnostics).

This information is collected by Google as an independent service provider/controller for advertising. We do not receive your game scores or device identifiers for our own use, and we never combine ad data with any personal profile (we hold none). The App contains no analytics SDK and no tracking SDK other than the Google Mobile Ads (AdMob) SDK described here.

3. How We Use Information

We use information only for these limited purposes:

  • To run the game — saving your progress, scores, settings, and unlocks on your device.
  • To keep the game free — showing advertising through Google AdMob.
  • To respect your choices — remembering your sound/haptics/theme and your ad‑consent selection.

We do not use your information for profiling unrelated to ads, we do not sell or rent it, and we do not show ads during active gameplay.

4. Advertising & Third‑Party Services

The only third‑party service integrated in the App is Google AdMob (including Google’s User Messaging Platform for consent). Ads help us offer the game at no cost. Google’s handling of data is governed by Google’s own policies:

  • Google Privacy Policy
  • How Google uses information from sites or apps that use its services
  • AdMob & data collection

Personalized vs. non‑personalized ads. By default the App requests non‑personalized ads. Where required by law (for example, for users in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, and Switzerland), the App shows a Google‑certified consent message the first time you play, and your choice determines whether personalized ads are shown. You can change this choice at any time (see Section 5).

Rewarded ads are optional. Some ads (for example, to revive a game, unlock a cosmetic theme, or get an extra undo) are opt‑in — they play only if you choose to watch them, and they never gate core gameplay.

5. Your Choices & Controls

  • Manage ad consent. Where the consent message applies, you can reopen it from Settings → Manage ad consent inside the App to review or change your selection.
  • Reset or limit your advertising ID. On Android, go to Settings → Google → Ads to reset your advertising ID or opt out of ad personalization. On iOS, use Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking and Apple Advertising.
  • Control in‑app data. You can turn off sound, haptics, and motion in Settings, and you can remove all on‑device data by uninstalling the App.

6. Legal Bases (EEA / UK)

If you are in the EEA, the UK, or Switzerland, we rely on the following legal bases under the GDPR/UK‑GDPR:

  • Legitimate interests — to operate the game and to serve non‑personalized advertising needed to keep it free.
  • Consent — for personalized advertising and any use of device identifiers that requires it. You may withdraw consent at any time via the controls in Section 5.

7. Your Rights

Depending on where you live (for example under the GDPR/UK‑GDPR, or the California Consumer Privacy Act), you may have rights to access, correct, delete, or restrict processing of your personal information, to object to certain processing, and to data portability. California residents have the right to know, delete, and opt out of the “sale” or “sharing” of personal information — note that we do not sell or share personal information for cross‑context behavioral advertising for our own benefit.

Because we do not maintain accounts or servers and cannot identify you from the App, the most direct way to exercise deletion of on‑device data is to uninstall the App or clear its data. For advertising data, use the Google controls in Section 5. For any other request, email us at solvemash@gmail.com and we will respond as required by applicable law.
